Aqaba Bay

Four countries share Aqaba Bay. The Jordanian part is 26 km long; it spreads from the Jordanian border to the Saudi Arabian border. On the seashore you can find free beaches, hotel beaches, the port of the Royal Yacht Club, several cargo ship ports, and a protected area which belongs to the University.

The fauna of the Aqaba Bay is very colorful, full of undersea life, so beautiful that it reminds us — a paradise could exist also on Earth.

Famous Dive Sites

Japanese Garden

Most Popular

The most known diving place, called Japanese Garden for its fascinating fauna. Here you can observe fantastic fishes with unbelievable colors — butterflyfish, scorpionfish, lionfish, cornet fish, stonefish, clown fish, grey moray — and the miraculous water life. You can also find hard and soft corals of many colors and shapes, giant gorgon, plant animals and many more life-forms.

9-27m

Cedar Pride Wreck

A 70m+ international cargo ship, sunken in 1985 to create a site for wreck divers. Situated onto two big coral reefs so divers can swim under it. The ship lies at its left side, parallel to the seashore, surrounded by beautiful corals, giant shells and various fishes.

Deep

C-130 Hercules

A spectacular artificial wreck of a C-130 Hercules transport aircraft, submerged to create a unique diving experience in the Red Sea waters of Aqaba.

Varied

The Tank

An anti-aircraft vehicle known as "the tank," another fascinating artificial reef that has become home to vibrant marine life and colorful coral formations.

Our Story

About Sea Guard

Yazan Alsaed PADI Instructor

Sea Guard offers diving and snorkeling trips at daytime as well as romantic trips at sunset. Founded in 2003, headquartered in Aqaba, the only seashore city of Jordan.

The beginnings of Sea Guard date back to 1993 when one young enthusiast, Yazan Alsaed, started to dive. He made his first open water dive exam in 1994 and in 1996 got divemaster degree. He worked as assistant instructor since 1998 and since 2000 he has been PADI open water dive instructor.

In 2003, Yazan Alsaed formally established Sea Guard as a private company. Years of experience allow us to suggest safe conditions for the trips. We organize our trips in small groups to provide high quality of services for our clients.

PADI Certified

The largest diving training organization in the world, presented in more than 175 countries. PADI diver training is based on gradual teaching — from Open Water Diver to Advanced, Specialty, Medic First Aid and Rescue Diver. Anywhere you go on your diver trips, local diving communities recognize your PADI license worldwide.
